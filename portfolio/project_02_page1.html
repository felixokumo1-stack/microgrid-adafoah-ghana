01 // Problem & Site

Siamekome Island — a representative community in the Volta River estuary, Ada East District — has no grid connection and no feasible path to grid extension. Approximately 500 households plus five anchor loads (health clinic, two schools, productive use enterprises, water pump) depend on intermittent diesel generation. A donor capital ceiling of EUR 500,000 and a reliability target of LPSP ≤ 5% define the design envelope. The decision question: what is the minimum-LCOE PV-wind-battery-diesel hybrid providing 24/7 electricity within these constraints?

02 // Load Profile

Bottom-up demand estimation using the IEA Multi-Tier Framework produced 249,660 kWh/year and a 51.5 kW peak demand. The profile exhibits a characteristic dual-peak: an anchor-load coincidence peak at 08:00 (clinic + schools + shops operating simultaneously) and a household evening peak at 18:00–21:00 as solar generation ceases. The evening peak is the critical battery sizing driver; the morning peak, occurring at partial solar irradiance, creates a transient battery discharge requirement even during daylight hours.

04 // Resource Assessment

PVGIS ERA5 TMY (2005–2020) confirmed a strong solar resource. Wind assessment required resolving a 32% discrepancy between ERA5 and MERRA-2 reanalysis products at 10 m height. ERA5 was selected as the primary basis after validation against West African coastal literature. A critical turbine-site mismatch was identified: the Vestas V80 reference turbine (rated speed 16 m/s, IEC Class I) produced only 4.2% CF versus 19.7% CF for an IEC Class III machine (rated speed 11 m/s) correctly matched to the 6.15 m/s hub-height regime. Solar–wind Pearson correlation of −0.24 confirmed partial complementarity driven by the ITCZ/SW monsoon seasonal cycle.
